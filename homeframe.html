About me

I am an interdisciplinary researcher between (micro)economics, psychology, and (decision) neuroscience, a NOMIS Professor of Decision Theory and Neuroeconomic Theory at the Zurich Center for Neuroeconomics, and Co-Editor in Chief of the Journal of Economic Psychology. I have also been full professor of Microeconomics at the Universities of Cologne and Konstanz, associate professor at the Universities of Vienna and Salamanca, and Visiting Professor at New York University-Abu Dhabi. I am from Spain, speak German fluently, and currently live in Switzerland.

I am interested in how humans make economic decisions, ranging from decisions under risk to strategic decisions (game theory) and voting. My research methods include formal-analytical modelling of bounded rationality, behavioral experiments, and decision neuroscience ("neuroeconomics"). I maintain a "Decisions and the Brain" blog at Psychology Today and a personal blog at Wordpress. I also co-organize the Economic Psychology Seminar of the IAREP.
